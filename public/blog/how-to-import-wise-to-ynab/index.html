How to Import Wise Transactions into YNAB, GnuCash & More

Published March 2026 · 5 min read

Wise (formerly TransferWise) is one of the most popular international banking services, but it does not offer direct OFX export — only PDF statements and XML (camt.053). This means importing your Wise transactions into apps like YNAB, GnuCash, or Quicken requires a conversion step. Here is exactly how to do it.

Step 1: Export Your Statement from Wise

  1. Log in to your Wise account at wise.com.
  2. Go to your USD (or other currency) account.
  3. Click "Statement" or "Download statement".
  4. Choose the date range you want.
  5. Select PDF format and download the file.

Wise also offers XML (camt.053) export for some accounts — if available, that works too and often gives cleaner results.

Step 2: Convert the Statement to OFX

  1. Open OFX Converter at ofx-converter.netlify.app.
  2. Drag and drop your Wise PDF (or XML) onto the upload area, or click to browse.
  3. Click "Convert to OFX".
  4. Once complete, click "Download OFX File".

The converter processes everything in your browser — your financial data is never sent to any server.

Step 3: Import into Your App

YNAB

In YNAB, go to your account → click the import icon → select your .ofx file → review and approve the transactions.

GnuCash

Go to File → Import → Import OFX/QFX → select your file. GnuCash will match transactions to your accounts automatically.

Quicken

Go to File → Import → OFX File → select your downloaded file and follow the prompts.

HomeBank

Go to File → Import → select the OFX file. HomeBank will add the transactions to the selected account.

Tips for Best Results

  • Download one month at a time to avoid duplicates when importing.
  • Review the converted transactions before importing — PDF conversion uses text detection which works well but may occasionally miss a transaction.
  • If using XML (camt.053) export from Wise, the conversion is more accurate than PDF.
  • Always reconcile your account after importing to catch any discrepancies.
