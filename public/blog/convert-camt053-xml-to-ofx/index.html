How to Convert camt.053 XML Bank Statements to OFX

Published March 2026 · 4 min read

camt.053 (formally ISO 20022 Bank-to-Customer Statement) is the international XML standard for bank account statements. It is widely used by European banks, SEPA-compliant institutions, and global services like Wise. If your bank exports statements in camt.053 format, you can convert them to OFX in seconds — and import them into any personal finance software.

What is camt.053?

camt.053 is part of the ISO 20022 family of financial messaging standards. The "camt" stands for Cash Management, and version 053 specifically defines the Bank-to-Customer Statement message. Unlike a PDF, a camt.053 file is a structured XML document containing all transaction details in machine-readable form: dates, amounts, currency, transaction codes, and descriptions.

Which Banks Use camt.053?

camt.053 is the standard export format for many banks, including:

  • Wise (formerly TransferWise) — XML export option
  • Revolut (in some markets)
  • Most major European retail banks (Deutsche Bank, ING, Rabobank, etc.)
  • SEPA-compliant banks across the EU
  • Many Swiss banks (UBS, Credit Suisse successor)

How to Export camt.053 from Wise

  1. Log in to your Wise account.
  2. Go to your account (USD, EUR, or other currency).
  3. Click "Statement".
  4. Select "XML" as the format (not PDF).
  5. Choose your date range and download.

How to Convert camt.053 to OFX

  1. Open OFX Converter at ofx-converter.netlify.app.
  2. Drag and drop your camt.053 XML file onto the upload area.
  3. Click "Convert to OFX".
  4. Download the resulting OFX file.
  5. Import into YNAB, GnuCash, Quicken, or your app of choice.

The converter automatically detects the camt.053 format, reads the correct fields (Ntry elements, BookgDt dates, Amt values, CdtDbtInd for debit/credit), and produces a standard OFX file.

camt.053 vs PDF — Which Should I Use?

If your bank offers both PDF and XML (camt.053) export, always choose XML. The structured data in camt.053 results in more accurate conversion — correct amounts, dates, and transaction types every time. PDF conversion uses text extraction which works well but can occasionally miss transactions in complex layouts.
